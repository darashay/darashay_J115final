About me

My name is Dara Shay and I’m a 20-year-old college student. Currently, I reside in my hometown of Palm Springs, California with my mom and cat but I attend the University of California, Berkeley. At Berkeley, I am a rising junior studying environmental policy and working to earn a double minor in Public Policy and Journalism. I also do work-study at the Graduate Assembly as an administrative assistant.

I would describe myself as an adventurous and friendly person. I'm constantly fascinated by the people I meet and I love to travel around so that I can try new food, meet new friends, and experience unique beauty around the world. Currently, I'm saving money and planning to travel abroad next summer hopefully.

I grew up in a single-parent household and my mom was always the person who took me traveling when I was younger. She inspired me to be more independent and seek opportunities for myself.

My Interests

In my spare time I like to get involved in extracurriculars that are important to me. My Jewish identity and heritage is a significant portion of my experience in college. I work with other students at Hillel to make Jewish life at Berkeley more vibrant. I go with friends to barbecues every Wednesday, candle lightings and Shabbat dinners every Friday evening, and holidays.

I also enjoy hanging out with my mom and my friends when I can and grabbing coffee, getting brunch, cooking, or roadtripping. During current times, I take hanging out in any form that means being safe.

Above are some of the ways that I have been spending the months since I've been away from Berkeley. I've been learning new recipes, like shakshuka, which is a Jewish dish. I've also been spending time with my mom and social distancing with one of my best friends since high school, Athena.
